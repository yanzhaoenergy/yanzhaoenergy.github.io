I am a Senior Engineer at Gas Technology Institute (GTI Energy), a nonprofit research institute in the Chicago area. I earned my Ph.D. in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering from the University of California, Irvine. My research focuses on renewable and fossil-fuel energy systems at the intersection of thermal–fluid sciences, environmental impact assessment, and community-scale technology deployment. I investigate both conventional fossil fuels (e.g., natural gas, propane, and coal) and low-carbon or renewable fuels (e.g., hydrogen, biogas, ammonia, and methanol), as well as electricity-integrated energy systems including microgrids, fuel cells, heat pumps, IC/gas turbine engines, and solar photovoltaic technologies.

  • Thermal-Fluid Sciences: Fuel interchangeability (natural gas, hydrogen, ammonia, biogas, etc.), Combustion, Electrolysis
  • Environmental Impacts: Emissions reduction (NOₓ, CO, etc.), Indoor air quality
  • Technology Deployment: Microgrids, Pipeline gas blending, Disadvantaged communities engagement
